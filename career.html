Work I've done

During highschool, I worked full-time as a housekeeper at the Hilton. It wasn't glamorous, but it taught me a lot about life. From talking to peers to clients. Dealing with difficult, unpleasant and unfair situations. Like most of my work experience, I wouldn't trade it for anything.

After the Hilton, I worked for a local extrusion factory. I started as a line worker, which was a role I filled off and on during the almost two years I worked there. I was quickly introduced to shipping and handling, and after a few months was the shipping and handling manager. After a year, I was the equivalent of the plant manager, dealing with production schedules, managing outgoing shipments and dealing with daily issues.

After that job, I worked in the cell tower industry for a year, specifically I did structural modification with a small team. The work was demanding, but fulfilling. I learned more in the year I spent on towers than I did anywhere else.
